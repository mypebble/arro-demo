Power from its premium-grade single-cylinder Briggs & Stratton four-stroke engine is sent to the drive-wheels via a six-speed Transmatic Shift-on-the-Go transmission, which makes adjusting the ground-speed more intuitive and less time-consuming than with a standard manual drive. The drive wheels are larger than the front wheels for easier turning and improved traction on damp grass.

Thanks to its three-in-one design, whereby you can choose between bagging; direct discharge; and mulching; the Lawnflite Mini Rider 60RDE Ride-On Mower is capable of achieving superb results on any grass-type, from a neat, manicured lawn, through to a less well maintained informal area (the mulch-kit and rear deflector are optional)

A five-step height-of-cut, adjustable from 38 to 95mm, allows you to adapt to changing weather- and grass-conditions; while a high-capacity 150-litre grass-collector means more time can be spent actually mowing with less time wasted at the compost-pile.

With a cutting-width of just 60cm and an ultra-tight 46cm turning-circle, the Lawnflite Mini Rider 60RDE will take awkward corners and closely spaced obstacles in its stride.

It has an adjustable seat, so you can sit in a position thats most comfortable for your stature; and it features Lawnflites practical step-through frame that means even the less nimble of us will find it easy and safe to get on and off.

Sport England Inspired Facilities
  • £150,000 Cash Award
  • Complex A few weeks
  • 35 days 04/02/2016

Sport England welcomes applications from education establishments, local authorities, sports clubs, and community organisations in England for funding to support community sport facilities. The Inspired Facilities programme will refurbish and upgrade club facilities, and convert existing buildings into venues suitable for community sport

Read More
What is it?

Inspired Facilities is focused on making it easier for local community and volunteer groups to improve and refurbish sports clubs or transform non- sporting venues into modern grassroots sport facilities.

Tired, worn out, old facilities make it harder for people to start or keep doing sport. They can also be more expensive to run and difficult to maintain. The Inspired Facilities fund could help you refurbish or upgrade your sports facility; or convert an existing building into a venue suitable for sport and accessible to the whole community.

Through Inspired Facilities, Sport England will invest in most types of improvement or refurbishment work that will help you develop sport in your local area.

Grants are available from 20,000 to 150,000

Your application should show how your project will keep and, ideally, attract more people to sport and that your project is needed and wanted by the local community.

Who is Eligible?

Applications can be submitted by organisations entitled to receive public funding, voluntary or community organisations, local authorities, sports clubs, playing field associations and education establishments in England.

You are expected to have partnership funding from a non-lottery source. Schools or educational establishments will, however, be expected to provide pound-for-pound partnership funding.

What Will I Get?

Community and voluntary organisations, including parish and town councils can apply for grants worth between 20,000 and 75,000. At least 70% of the available funding available is set aside for these groups.

Local authorities, statutory bodies and* education establishments can apply for grants between 20,000 and 150,000. *

What Next?

As a result of feedback from previous applicants, Sport England have changed the application process for Inspired Facilities. It is now an open programme which means that you can submit your application as soon as you are ready rather than having to rush to send it in by a specific date.

Decision meetings will then take place twice a year, the next one of these will be in September 2015 when all applications received prior to the 8 June will be assessed. The second will take place in April 2016 for applications received before 2 January. Please note only one application per organisation can be considered at each decision meeting.

A mini website is available, full of information to help you with your application.

Good luck!

  • SC Sports Center

    Our project is the creation of an Sports Field for the children that surround our school. The Sports Center will be used by teachers, care providers and parents to facilitate investigation and reflective time for children who suffer from autism and behavioural issues. It will provide stimulation to all types of learners allowing for differentiation and encouragement of personalised learning treating each child as an individual.

    We are planning to redevelop an unusable area of schools grounds (9m x 7m) into the Exploration Deck. A consultation was held with local stakeholders including council and community members, staff, and parents about the needs and priorities of those children managing behavioural issues and autism. The outcome of this consultation found a lack of facilities and resources for play-based approaches to developing more positive child/parent relationships.

    We aim to achieve this goal by engineering our surroundings to incorporate colors, sounds, scents and textures stimulate the five senses. This will provide a safe, calm, outdoor environment and appeal to all age groups. The Exploration Deck will be able to be accessed in all weather conditions as we plan to cover the area with a shelter. We've already done some preparatory work to the grounds and started fundraising to cover the costs of benches, a water fountain and outdoor musical instruments. Planters will be installed around the perimeter of the area to grow specific sensory plants including lavender, bamboo and mint which will create a fragrance very specific to the area. The floor surface will provide different textures and colours like green artificial grass, blue soft pour and brown wood chip making it appealing to the eye.
